Hi, I'm Anya —
the face behind the food

Welcome to my page! I'm Anya, a college student, food lover, and self-proclaimed kitchen experimenter based in Boston. I started @eatsbyanya in high school as an informal way to share the meals I was already making — the ones that were too good not to post. This site acts as my own personal diary where I collect all my favorites so you can make them too. Whether it's a planned out intricate Thanksgiving spread, or a quick 10-min meal, there is something available for everyone!

My Story

My love for cooking started long before I knew what I was doing. Growing up, my dad was the king of "fun" meals with elaborate, over-the-top dinners that turned a regular Tuesday into an event. My mom, on the other hand, was the keeper of our South Asian roots, making the kind of traditional dishes that felt like home in every bite. I grew up watching both of them and absorbing everything, even if I didn't realize it at the time. I'm still learning from both of them — and I have a long way to go before I come close.


The real turning point was COVID. Suddenly stuck at home as a high school freshman with nothing but time, I turned the kitchen into my playground. Every day was a new experiment — some disasters, some surprises, mostly baked goods that my family was very patient in taste-testing. It was messy and imperfect, but I was hooked.


Somewhere along the way, cooking stopped being something I just did for myself and became something I owned. I became the resident chef, the one volunteering to cook Thanksgiving dinner, showing up to family reunions with something homemade, taking over the kitchen at Christmas just because I wanted to. With friends, a night in meant a four-course meal that nobody asked for but everybody showed up for. I loved feeding people. I still do. It's honestly more of an ego boost to slave away in the kitchen for hours and then have everybody complimenting you on how amazing it was. Bonus was that in my family, whoever cooked did not have to clean.


By sophomore and junior year, I wanted to do more than just cook: I wanted to share. I built my first food blog for a computer science class. It was rough around the edges (you can check it out here for a good laugh), but it planted a seed. Soon after, I moved to Instagram and TikTok, hoping something would take off. Instagram grew slowly, but TikTok was a different story. The micro-engagement, the comments asking for recipes, the feeling that people actually wanted what I was making — that was the energy that kept me going. TikTok became my main home, though I still show up on both.


College, though? Boston's smallest kitchen, a busy schedule, and a feeling that everything I was making had started to blend together. I hit a rut, and posting quietly fell away. This site is my way of holding myself accountable. A place to collect every recipe, document every meal, and keep showing up even when it's just avocado toast on a random Wednesday morning. Because at the end of the day, cooking has always been how I connect: to my roots, to my family and friends, and to anyone who finds their way here.


What You'll Find Here

From quick 30-minute weeknight dinners to slow weekend cooking projects, you'll find a little bit of everything here. I love playing with bold flavors across cuisines — a lot of Asian-inspired bowls, Mexican classics with a twist, Italian comfort food, and plenty of American staples done right. Most recipes land somewhere between easy and medium difficulty, because food should be approachable without being boring. Whether you're a college student cooking in a tiny kitchen or just someone who wants to eat well without overthinking it, there's something here for you.
